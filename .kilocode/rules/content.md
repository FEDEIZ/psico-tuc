# Content Rules

The psychologist's landing page must convey professionalism, trust, and empathy. Follow these content guidelines to ensure consistency and effectiveness.

## Tone & Voice
- **Professional yet approachable**: Use clear, warm language that reassures visitors.
- **Avoid jargon**: Explain psychological terms in plain language.
- **Empathetic**: Acknowledge the visitor's potential struggles without being patronizing.
- **Confidentiality**: Emphasize privacy and safety (e.g., "Your sessions are completely confidential").

## Required Sections
1. **Hero / Header**
   - Psychologist's full name and credentials (e.g., "Dr. Jane Smith, PhD, Licensed Clinical Psychologist").
   - A clear value proposition (e.g., "Supporting your mental wellness journey").
   - Primary call‑to‑action button (e.g., "Book a Free Consultation").

2. **About the Psychologist**
   - Brief biography highlighting education, licenses, and experience.
   - Personal touch: mention the therapist's approach (CBT, mindfulness, etc.).
   - Professional headshot (smiling, approachable).

3. **Services**
   - List of therapy types (individual, couples, family, group).
   - Specialties (anxiety, depression, trauma, stress, etc.).
   - Each service should have a short description.

4. **Why Choose Me / Trust Signals**
   - Professional memberships (APA, state licensing board).
   - Testimonials (with client initials and a photo if permitted).
   - Statistics (e.g., "15+ years of experience", "500+ clients helped").

5. **How It Works**
   - Step‑by‑step explanation of the therapy process (initial contact, first session, ongoing support).
   - Address common concerns (cost, insurance, frequency of sessions).

6. **Contact & Location**
   - Contact form with minimal fields (name, email, message).
   - Phone number, email address, physical address.
   - Embedded map (Google Maps) and office hours.

7. **FAQ**
   - Common questions about therapy, confidentiality, fees, insurance, etc.
   - Answers should be concise and reassuring.

8. **Footer**
   - Repeat essential contact information.
   - Links to privacy policy, terms of service.
   - Social media icons (LinkedIn, Psychology Today, etc.).
   - Copyright notice.

## Legal & Ethical Requirements
- **Disclaimer**: Include a disclaimer stating that the website is for informational purposes only and not a substitute for professional medical advice.
- **Privacy Policy**: Must be accessible and explain how client data is protected (HIPAA compliance for US).
- **Terms of Service**: Outline the terms of using the website.
- **Accessibility Statement**: Optional but recommended.

## Call‑to‑Action (CTA) Strategy
- At least two primary CTAs: one in the hero, one before the footer.
- Use action‑oriented language: "Start Healing Today", "Get in Touch", "Schedule Your Free Call".
- Secondary CTAs can offer lead magnets (e.g., "Download a free stress‑management guide").

## Visual Content
- Use high‑quality, calming images (office interior, nature scenes, abstract patterns).
- Avoid clichéd stock photos that feel impersonal.
- Ensure all images are optimized for web (see performance rules).
